## Design Context

### Users
Korean office workers (직장인) coordinating team schedules. Desktop-primary, used during work hours. Mix of roles: admin, team leads, members. Functional, goal-oriented usage — they want to see and create schedule information quickly, not admire the interface.

### Brand Personality
**3 words**: precise · quiet · functional  
Like a well-made paper planner: warm, intentional, trustworthy. Not cold corporate, not playful startup. The kind of product someone uses every day without noticing how good it feels.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Theme**: Light  
**Visual tone**: Warm editorial — ivory surfaces, charcoal type, amber accents. Inspired by premium physical stationery and Japanese office design. Anti-references: generic SaaS (blue + gradient), glassmorphism, "AI purple/cyan", gradient backgrounds.  
**Typography**: Pretendard (Korean geometric, loaded via CDN) for UI text. Clean hierarchy with strong weight contrast.  
**Color palette**: Warm off-white base (#FAFAF8), near-black text (#1C1917), warm stone borders (#E7E5E4), amber accent (#B45309) — explicitly not blue-heavy.

### Design Principles
1. **Warmth over clinical** — even technical elements (dates, times, codes) use warm neutrals, never cold gray
2. **Restraint** — no gradients, no shadows that glow, no borders that decorate for decoration's sake
3. **Type does the work** — hierarchy through weight + size, not color or decoration
4. **Functional first** — every visual element serves the task (finding, creating, sharing schedules)
5. **Korean context** — Korean labels feel native, not translated; spacing accounts for Korean character density
